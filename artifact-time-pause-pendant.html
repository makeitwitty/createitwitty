The Story

This pendant emerged from a peculiar frustration: the constant awareness of time without any control over it. I found myself checking the time obsessively, yet never having enough of it. The solution wasn't more precise timekeeping, but the opposite‚Äîa reminder to step outside time entirely.

The mechanism inside doesn't keep accurate time. It runs fast, then slow, sometimes backwards. The hands move at their own pace, creating a rhythm that has nothing to do with productivity or schedules. It's deliberately unreliable as a timepiece, but perfectly reliable as a prompt to pause.

The weight is intentional‚Äîheavy enough to remind you it's there, light enough to wear comfortably. When you catch yourself checking it automatically, you're reminded that this moment exists outside the tyranny of precise timing. It's not about telling time, it's about taking time.

Made from brass that develops its own patina over months of wear, each pendant becomes more personal with use. The chain length allows it to rest naturally over the heart, creating a subtle physical reminder of presence over productivity.

Materials & Construction

Primary Material Solid Brass

Each case is hand-turned from solid brass, with the movement modified to create irregular timing. The hands are weighted differently to move at unpredictable speeds, making conventional time-reading impossible.

The chain is assembled link by link, with each connection tested for durability. The brass develops a natural patina over time‚Äîsome prefer to polish it regularly, others let it age naturally to deep bronze tones.

Living With It

Wear it when you need reminding that not everything operates on a schedule. The unpredictable hands become a gentle joke about precision, a physical reminder that some moments deserve to exist outside measured time.

It's not jewelry in the decorative sense‚Äîit's a functional piece for disrupting time anxiety. Some people wear it daily, others save it for particularly scheduled days when they need reminding that not everything has to be optimized.

The brass warms against skin and develops its own character through wear. The movement never stops, creating a quiet ticking that becomes comforting background rhythm. It ages beautifully, each scratch and patina mark adding to its story.
